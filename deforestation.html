What is Deforestation?

This is the link

Deforestation is the action of the removal of trees. Human action in which people cut down trees or other sorts of vegetation in the forests is one cause of deforestation. Wildfires can also be a cause of it too. Wildfires have a negative impact on species in which their habitats can be lost in fires.

Why does deforestation affect animals?

Animals that live out in the wild need a habitat that is suitable for them. This means areas where they are secure, comfortable and safe. These habitats are their homes which are used just like people use their homes. Animals also use them to escape predators. However, when their habitat or area is destroyed due to deforestation, animals lose their access to foods that were found in that area that was destroyed. This leads to animals exposed to more threats like predators. According to the stand for trees, “we lose around 137 species every day” because of deforestation.

The Effect of Deforestation on The Environment.

Deforestation has also been shown to be one of the causes of global warming. This is because trees absorb and store carbon dioxide during photosynthesis. The carbon is chemically in the wood of trees. So when trees are burned the carbons is brought back to the atmosphere as carbon dioxide. Since there is less and less trees they can't absorb the carbon dioxide. So the carbon dioxide accumulates in the atomosphere increasing global warming. This is because carbon dixide absorbs the heat in which earth had radiated of the planet. More and more carbon dixide equals hotter and hotter temperature. According to the World Wildlife Fund forest loss causes around 10% of global warming. This in return, causes changes in weather world wide. Animals have a harder time maintaining their needs when climate changes. Animals are forced to migrate because of it.

Pygmy Elephants

Asian pygmy and African elephants can grow to be at the most 11,000 pounds. Even though they weigh a ton they are known to be the smallest elephants. They are in danger of having major competition with other forest elephants for homes they could live in. Pygmy elephants' homes are being turned into farms, etc. Lastly, their tusks are targeted by hunters. They are endangered, according to the trees there are only 30,000 pygmy elephants left.

Sloths are famous for how slow they are. However, the pygmy sloths (Pygmy Three-Toed sloths) are known to be exceptional swimmers. This species can be found off the coast of Panama on a small island and can’t be found anywhere else. Although not many people live there, fishers still trim down a tree called mangroves.
