What is Digital Citizenship?



Here is a video briefly talking about digital citizenship

Digital Citizenship tells us how we should act and embody when using technology. This way of acting allows us to protect ourselves from the dangers online. It's also used to respect others' rights as human beings.

Council of Europe says that digital citizenship can be shown as....

“The competent and positive engagement with digital technologies (creating, working, sharing, socializing, investigating, playing, communicating, and learning); participating actively and responsibly (values, skills, attitudes, knowledge) in communities (local, national, global) at all levels (political, economic, social, cultural, and intercultural); being involved in a double process of lifelong learning (in formal, informal, and non-formal settings) and continuously defending human dignity”[4].

Why digital citizenship is important (more specifically)

There is a good amount of news and media that is misleading and provides misinformation. Some people spread misinformation and or disinformation and are taking advantage of people who are uneducated with technological knowledge. Digital Citizenship Education is needed to ensure protection to others from being vulnerable to these issues. Digital citizenship in other words is using the internet safely and responsible. It promotes human rights like freedom, security and privacy. Thus, digital citizenship thrives confidence and a positive environment towards the internet rather than negativity. So treating people with respect and kindness through the internet. Whereas cyberbullying would be bad digital citizenship.

What is good digital citizenship online?


A good digital citizen protects their personal information. Meaning they wouldn’t share personal information like address, security number and so forth. A good digital citizen would treat others with the same respect as they would in person. This includes commenting on social media, sending an email and so forth. Good Digital citizenship is to use the internet safely and responsible. When we practice good digital citizenship we make it a welcoming place for everyone online.

What is bad digital citizenship?


Bad citizenship would include anything that would hurt others. Also, misinformation or disinformation. A example of bad digital citzenship includes cyberbullying. Cyberbullying people can be dishonest and untrustworthy for personal gains. They use different types of verbal abuse to engage in disrectful behavior online. Other examples of bad citizenship includes spreading hatred to others or harassing others online. Good citizenship online can be taught.
